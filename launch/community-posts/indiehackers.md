## Post Body (ready to paste)
I've been building Beam, a privacy-first analytics product, in public with an autonomous agent loop that ships one scoped story at a time.

Current state:
- Launch page: https://beam-privacy.com/product-hunt?ref=indiehackers&utm_source=indiehackers&utm_medium=community&utm_campaign=launch_kit_apr_2026&utm_content=founder_post
- Stack: Cloudflare Workers, D1, KV, Stripe, Resend
- Product includes: pageviews, events, goals, channels, API, alerts, weekly insights, public dashboards, and a live demo

What worked:
- Keeping stories small enough for one implementation pass kept shipping velocity high
- Dogfooding on Beam's own site helped catch instrumentation gaps
- Tight, reusable codebase patterns reduced regression risk as scope grew

What didn't work yet:
- Distribution lagged product progress
- I now have a community-post launch kit and am prioritizing traffic + conversion learning

I'd appreciate feedback from other founders:
- If you had this product today with zero users, what would be your first 3 distribution moves?
- Would you position this as "privacy analytics" first or "simple decision support analytics" first?
